# 🏛️ Today in History


⚔️ 229 – Sun Quan proclaims himself emperor of Eastern Wu.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_Quan)

🏰 1266 – War of Saint Sabas: In the Battle of Trapani, the Venetians defeat a larger Genoese fleet, capturing all its ships.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_of_Saint_Sabas)

📜 1305 – A peace treaty between the Flemish and the French is signed at Athis-sur-Orge.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flanders)

🛡️ 1532 – Henry VIII of England and Francis I of France sign the "Treaty of Closer Amity With France" (also known as the Pommeraye treaty), pledging mutual aid against Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_VIII)

⚓ 1611 – The mutinous crew of Henry Hudson's fourth voyage sets Henry, his son and seven loyal crew members adrift in an open boat in what is now Hudson Bay; they are never heard from again.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Hudson)

🚀 1713 – The French residents of Acadia are given one year to declare allegiance to Britain or leave Nova Scotia, Canada.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acadia)

💼 1951 – The ocean liner SS United States is christened and launched.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SS_United_States)

🎖️ 1959 – Convicted Manhattan Project spy Klaus Fuchs is released after only nine years in prison and allowed to emigrate to Dresden, East Germany where he resumes a scientific career.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manhattan_Project)

📡 1860 – The United States Congress establishes the Government Printing Office.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Congress)

🏙️ 1812 – War of 1812: Great Britain revokes the restrictions on American commerce, thus eliminating one of the chief reasons for going to war.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_of_1812)

⚙️ 2014 – The last of Syria's declared chemical weapons are shipped out for destruction.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_chemical_weapons_program)

💥 1940 – Henry Larsen begins the first successful west-to-east navigation of Northwest Passage from Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Larsen_(explorer))

🕊️ 2012 – Ashton Eaton breaks the decathlon world record at the United States Olympic Trials.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashton_Eaton)

⚔️ 1968 – Seventy-four people were killed and 150 other injured in a stampede at a football match between Boca Juniors and Club Atlético River Plate in Buenos Aires, Argentina.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Tragedy_of_Gate_12)

🏰 1972 – Title IX of the United States Civil Rights Act of 1964 is amended to prohibit sexual discrimination to any educational program receiving federal funds.. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Title_IX)
